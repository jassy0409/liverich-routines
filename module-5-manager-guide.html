Negotiation & Retention — how to run the training

Objective: teach the negotiation ladder (value → bonus → discount-as-favor), speed and vibe discipline, and the four retention levers that keep whales.

🔒
Managers only — contains the exam answers. Chatters get the Chatter Edition.

Introduction — what to say

"Two topics today because they're really one topic: keeping value. Negotiation is keeping the value of the content — never discount first. Retention is keeping the value of the fan — most of our revenue comes from a small group of regulars, and they stay for memory, consistency, chemistry, and feeling special. One boundary before we start: we persuade with connection. Guilt, pressure, and manufactured emergencies are off the table — always."

Coach's notes by topic

The ladder

Acknowledge and hold value → add a bonus instead of cutting price → small discount only as a last-resort favor. Roleplay it: you play the hesitating fan, make them climb down the ladder in order.

🚫Watch for: the panic discount — price cut in the first reply. It trains fans to always push back.

Speed + vibe

Hesitating fans jump the queue — every silent minute is a chance to change his mind. And the tone never goes cold-transactional; the negotiation lives inside the flirtation.

🎯Key takeaway: a negotiation is the most time-sensitive conversation on the shift.

Retention & the no-sell

Memory (fan notes!), consistency (implicit appointments), chemistry (insiders), exclusivity. Then the counterintuitive one: after a big purchase, days of pure-relationship mode beat another pitch. Tie to the VIP-funnel pillar on the scorecard.

Answer key — Module 5 exam

  • 1C — First move on "can you do less?": acknowledge and offer value — never an instant discount.
  • 2B — A discount is a last resort, framed as an exceptional personal favor.
  • 3A — Speed: a hesitating fan is a fan who can leave; negotiations are top priority.
  • 4D — Whales are a small share of fans and a large share of revenue — protect and prioritize them.
  • 5B — Strategic not-selling builds trust that pays back bigger on the next premium offer.

Written answers (Q6–15) — grade on

  • ✓Climbs the ladder in order; discount framed as a favor, never an opener.
  • ✓Stays warm and in-voice through the negotiation.
  • ✓Uses remembered details (notes) naturally in retention replies.
  • ✓The no-sell message genuinely sells nothing.

Reflection — send after training

  • 1Check your last three negotiations: which rung did you start on?
  • 2Name your top three spenders and one personal detail about each — without opening notes. If you can't, that's the lesson.
  • 3Plan one no-sell touchpoint for a whale this week.
