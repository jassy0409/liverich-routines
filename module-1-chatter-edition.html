Mastering the Comeback

Turning "no" into the next conversation. Don't memorize scripts — learn to understand why the fan is saying no. Every objection gives you information.

Before you start

Your job is to identify the obstacle and keep the conversation moving. There are only four obstacles: price, timing, content, and stalling. Learn to name which one you're looking at before you type.

💰 Price objections

"That's too expensive."

What he usually means

"I like it, but not at this price." He replied instead of ignoring you — that's interest.

Fanthat's expensive
✕ Don'tokay babe ❤️
✓ Betteri totally get it ❤️ that's my biggest bundle. i have something smaller i think you'd enjoy just as much

🎯 Goal — find another path to yes.

⏰ Timing objections

"Maybe later / next Friday."

What he usually means

"I'm interested, just not today." That's a booking, not a rejection.

Fani'll buy it next friday
✕ Don'tokay.
✓ Betterperfect ❤️ i'll save it for you. message me friday and i'll have it ready

🎯 Goal — book the next opportunity. Ask when payday is and note it.

🎥 Content objections

"I already have something like that."

What he usually means

"Show me something different." He's not rejecting spending — just that content.

Fani already bought something like that
✕ Don'ttrust me, this one's different
✓ Betterwhat are you in the mood to see today? i'll find something you don't have yet ❤️

🎯 Goal — find what he really wants. Ask before the next PPV.

💬 Stalling

"Haha maybe 😜"

What he usually means

He's enjoying the chat but not moving. You have to lead.

Fanhaha maybe
✕ Don't(chat for 30 more minutes with no ask)
✓ Betterno pressure ❤️ i just thought you'd enjoy this one. want me to send it over?

🎯 Goal — lead the conversation. If he still doesn't move, exit politely and keep it warm.

The psychology of a "no"

Before the techniques, understand what an objection actually is — because your emotional read of it decides everything you type next.

  • 1A reply is interest. A fan who truly doesn't want it ignores you. The moment he types "too expensive" or "maybe later," he has invested energy in the conversation — silence is the real rejection, an objection is a door left open.
  • 2He's telling you the obstacle. Price, timing, content, or momentum. He rarely says it directly, but it's always in there. Your first job is diagnosis, not persuasion.
  • 3Don't take it personally. Chatters who feel rejected either go cold ("okay.") or push hard (needy). Both lose. The objection is about his wallet, his week, or his mood — not about you.
  • 4Your reply speed matters. An objection answered in seconds feels like a conversation; answered in twenty minutes it becomes a decision he already finalized alone.

Thread 3 · Stalling — one ask, then the warm exit

Fanhaha maybe, we'll see 😜
Model · one confident askno pressure ❤️ i just know you'd love this one. want me to send it over?
Fanhaha maybe later
Model · exit warm, keep the relationshipokay okay, i'll stop tempting you 😌 tell me about your day instead, how did that job interview thing go?

Two "maybes" means the sale isn't today. The warm exit costs nothing and keeps him coming back — a third push would cost the fan.

Thread 4 · Stacked objections — when he objects twice

Fanthat's a lot… and honestly i have stuff like that already
Model · the second objection is the real onemm so it's not really the price, you just want something you haven't seen before 😏 fair. tell me what you're actually in the mood for and i'll surprise you

Modeloh i have EXACTLY the thing. give me two minutes 😈

When objections stack, the last one is usually the true one. Diagnose again instead of discounting — he told you the problem was content, not money.

Common mistakes

  • 1Ending the conversation after the first objection.
  • 2Discounting immediately without understanding the problem.
  • 3Sending another PPV without addressing the objection.
  • 4Assuming instead of asking questions.
  • 5Forgetting to follow up.

Golden rules

  • ★Every objection is information.
  • ★Don't react. Understand first.
  • ★Every reply should move the conversation forward.
  • ★Ask before assuming.
  • ★Always leave another path to yes.
