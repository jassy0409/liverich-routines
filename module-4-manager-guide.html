The Sale — how to run the training

Objective: teach the three-beat sequence (hook → reinforce → max two light follow-ups), selling the feeling instead of the file, and after-care between sales.

🔒
Managers only — contains the exam answers. Chatters get the Chatter Edition.

Introduction — what to say

"Today is the sale itself. Two big ideas. First: a script lives inside emotional continuity — the same pitch lands or dies depending on when it arrives. Second: we sell feelings, not files. 'New video 5 min' is a file. 'The one you'll be replaying tomorrow' is a feeling. And one house rule up front: no guilt ladders. Two light follow-ups maximum — the sad-emoji spam some agencies teach is a scored dead-end here."

Coach's notes by topic

The three beats

Hook with desire + exclusivity + a callback to the real conversation; reinforcement ~30s later to amplify anticipation; then at most two playful nudges. Have the room write a hook live for a sample context and read them aloud.

🚫Watch for: captions that describe content (length, acts, resolution) instead of the experience.

After-care between sales

The moment after a purchase is the highest-trust moment of the whole relationship — and the moment most chatters torch by instantly re-pitching. Demo the vending-machine vs relationship example from the Chatter Edition.

🎯Key takeaway: after-care funds the next pitch. The next pitch never funds itself.

Deliver what you promise

Over-hyping is borrowing from tomorrow: it becomes disputes, chargebacks, and dead fans. Curiosity yes, deception no. Chargeback ratio is an account-level risk — make that connection explicit.

Answer key — Module 4 exam

  • 1B — The hook is an invitation to an experience, not a description of the file.
  • 2C — The reinforcement amplifies anticipation while the moment is hot.
  • 3A — One or two light, in-tone follow-ups — never a guilt ladder.
  • 4D — After a purchase: nurture first. No instant re-pitch.
  • 5B — Selling feelings means describing how it will make him feel, personalized to him.

Written answers (Q6–15) — grade on

  • ✓Hooks create desire, exclusivity, and curiosity — no file specs.
  • ✓Follow-ups capped at two, playful, zero neediness or guilt.
  • ✓After-care message continues the relationship without a pitch.
  • ✓Nothing over-promises beyond what the vault actually contains.

Reflection — send after training

  • 1Rewrite your most-used caption as an experience instead of a file.
  • 2What's your current follow-up habit — and does it survive the two-nudge rule?
  • 3Recall a sale you re-pitched too fast. What would after-care have looked like?
