01 — The Mindset

You are not "selling." You are the most interesting person in her inbox.

A fan didn't subscribe for a transaction. He subscribed for a feeling — that a specific woman finds him worth her attention. Your job is to protect and deepen that feeling. Sales are what happens when you do it well.

Hold three ideas at once and everything else in this manual follows from them:

  • The relationship is the product. PPVs, tips and VIP are just the moments where the fan gets to spend on a relationship that already feels real. No relationship, no repeat spend — only regret and chargebacks.
  • He can tell when he's being processed. Copy-paste scripts, ignoring what he just said, pushing a locked photo the second he says hi — fans feel it instantly. The whole edge of a human chatter over a bot is that you actually read him.
  • Lifetime value beats today's number. A fan you over-pushed tonight is a fan who unsubscribes tomorrow. We are playing a long game: a whale who trusts you is worth more than fifty squeezed strangers.
North star

Before you send anything ask one question: "Does this make him feel more seen, or just more sold-to?" If it's the second, rewrite it.

02 — The Method

The IRAE Framework

Every profitable conversation moves through four stages. You don't rush them and you don't skip them — a fan pushed to stage four while still in stage one just closes the app. IRAE is the spine of the house style; internalize the order.

I
Identify

Identify — read the fan before you type

Who is this? New sub or returning whale? What did he say, what's his name, what's his timezone, what has he bought before? Check fan notes and history first. The opening line is different for a fresh trial link than for a VIP who tipped $200 last week.

Goal: know who you're talking to and what he's likely here for before your first reply.

R
Rapport

Rapport — earn the right to sell

Use his name. Ask a real question and react to the real answer. Find one true thing to connect over — his day, his job, the game he mentioned. This is the stage most chatters skip, and it's the one that makes every later sale easy. Two-way conversation, not a monologue with a price tag.

Goal: he feels like he's talking to a person who remembers him.

A
Attract / Arouse

Attract — build the tension, don't dump it

Now you raise the temperature: tease, hint, make him curious about what's behind the lock. Attraction is anticipation. You describe just enough that he wants more, and you let him ask. A tease that he chased is worth ten unlocks you shoved at him.

Goal: he is the one leaning in and asking "how much?"

E
Escalate

Escalate — land it, then ladder

Present the offer with confidence and a clean price. After a yes, ladder up — a bundle, an add-on, the next tier — while the moment is hot. After a satisfied done, stop. Escalation includes knowing when the sale is complete and switching back to warmth so he leaves happy and comes back.

Goal: the sale closes, value is maximized, and the relationship survives it.

Worked example

Watch IRAE run in one short thread. Notice the chatter never names a price until the fan is basically asking for it.

03 — The Craft

Best chatting technique

The habits that separate a top earner from a warm body with a keyboard. None of these are tricks — they're just what genuine, attentive attention looks like at scale.

Open a loop, don't close one

End messages that invite a reply. A question, a tease, a "guess what happened today" beats a statement he can't respond to. Every closed loop is a dead thread; every open loop is a fan still typing.

Match his energy, then lead it

If he's shy, be gentle first. If he's blunt and ready, don't waste his time. You mirror where he is, then you set the direction. Leading from the wrong starting point is why fans go cold.

Sell the experience, not the file

"A 4-minute video" is a file. "The one you'll be thinking about at your desk tomorrow" is an experience. Describe how it'll make him feel, never the resolution or the runtime alone.

Price with confidence, once

State the price plainly and stop typing. Don't apologize for it, don't stack three nervous messages, don't immediately discount. Confidence is itself part of the value. If he hesitates, ask what he was hoping for — don't just slash the number.

One ask at a time

Never send two locked offers back to back. It reads as a vending machine. Land one, breathe, reconnect, then ladder.

Remember and call back

He told you his dog's name three days ago? Ask about the dog. Callbacks are the single most powerful proof that he's talking to a person, not a rotation of strangers. Log it in fan notes so the next shift can do it too.

✓ Do

  • Use his name early and naturally
  • React to what he actually said
  • Tease before you present a price
  • Let him ask "how much?"
  • Ladder after a yes, while it's hot
  • Stop cleanly after a satisfied "done"
  • Write fan notes for the next shift

✕ Don't

  • Paste a script that ignores his last line
  • Drop a locked PPV on "hi"
  • Send two offers in a row
  • Guilt-trip or fake-sulk to pressure him
  • Over-promise content you can't deliver
  • Keep pushing after he's clearly satisfied
  • Go silent the second he's paid

04 — House Voices

Two models, two completely different people

The fan is talking to her, not to you. Voice discipline is non-negotiable — a wrong word breaks the illusion and it's what our scorecard grades hardest. Learn the account you're on cold before your first shift on it.

Shantal Monique

Personal · warm · one-to-one

Voice
Speaks like she's writing to one man she genuinely likes. Proper sentences, real punctuation, first names only.
Never uses
"Hey" / "hay", pet names (baby, babe, handsome), or crude anatomy shorthand. It's not who she is.
Feels like
A grounded, elegant woman giving you her full attention.

"Hi Marcus, I've been thinking about what you said last night. Tell me how your day actually went."

Karina

Playful · bratty · lowercase

Voice
Lowercase, teasing, a little bratty and fun. Fast, flirty energy.
Pet names
"babe / baby" are allowed only for a VIP who is actively spending in that moment — not as a default filler for everyone.
Feels like
The fun, slightly-too-confident girl who makes you work for it.

"oh you're back… i was starting to think you forgot about me 🙄 prove you didn't"

Graded

Restricted words are counted from your outgoing messages only and pull your score down. Shantal: no "hey/hay", no pet names, no crude terms. Karina: pet names only for an actively-spending VIP. When in doubt, stay in the safe register — you can always warm up, you can't un-send.

05 — The Funnel

Where the money actually is

Four revenue moments, in roughly the order a healthy fan moves through them. Your value-setting score is about landing these and laddering them without leaving money on the table or breaking trust.

PPVPay-per-view locked content sent in DMs.Tease first, sell the experience, price with confidence, present one at a time.
TipsFree-form spending — appreciation, requests, games.Give him easy reasons to spend on you, not just content. "Tip if you want me to…", tip menus, small games.

06 — Reading the Room

Buyer signals & avoiding dead ends

The highest-scoring chatters read intent and respond to it. The lowest ones follow a script off a cliff — pushing a satisfied fan, deflecting a real question, or re-poking someone who already left. Learn to read the signal under the words.

Dead ends

These four behaviors cost you the most on the scorecard and cost us fans: deflecting real questions into a pitch, over-pushing past a satisfied signal, withholding attention the moment he's paid, and needy guilt-trip re-pokes ("why are you ignoring me 🥺"). Avoiding these is a whole scoring pillar on its own.

07 — Compliance & Safety

The rules that keep the accounts alive

One careless message can get a model banned or a payment reversed. These aren't suggestions — they protect the creator's income and yours. Read OnlyFans' own policies (linked in Resources) and treat them as the floor.

Platform hard rules

  • Never move a fan off-platform. No requests to pay by other apps, meet in person, or take content elsewhere. It violates the Terms and kills our recourse if something goes wrong.
  • Nothing illegal or impersonating. No content involving minors (real or implied age-play framing), no non-consensual themes, no pretending to be someone you're not off-platform.
  • Only sell what exists and is cleared. Don't promise custom content the model hasn't agreed to. Release forms and consent are tracked for a reason.
  • Deliver what he paid for. Non-delivery is the fastest route to a chargeback and a dispute.

Chargeback discipline

A fan who feels tricked disputes the charge — and a high chargeback ratio threatens the whole account's standing with the platform. Honest teasing sells; misleading "bait and switch" (promising more than you send) creates disputes. Protect the ratio by never overselling.

Explicit content: how we handle it internally

Threads get explicit — that's the platform. Two rules govern how we treat that content in coaching and records:

  • Never reproduce explicit messages in reports, notes, or cards. Coaching scores a thread on its structure and outcome — who escalated, did the sale land, was a signal read — never by quoting the content.
  • Match, don't over-drive. Let the fan set the pace and meet him there. A chatter who pushes further than the fan wanted is both a trust risk and a coaching flag. Reading who's leading whom is a judgment you make every thread.
Golden rule

If you'd be uncomfortable with a manager reading the exact message back to you, don't send it. Escalate anything genuinely off (a fan mentioning being underage, threats, self-harm, real-world coercion) to your manager immediately — do not handle it alone.

08 — On Shift

How a shift actually runs

The operational side of the craft. Consistency across shifts is what makes a fan feel like he's talking to one continuous person, even though three chatters cover a day.

Start of shift

  • Read the handover / recent fan notes so you know where each active thread was left.
  • Work the VIP-tagged fans first — they're the priority and they notice speed.
  • Check what's already been sent from the scheduled queue so you don't repeat auto-content as if it were live.

Continuity & attribution

Shifts are covered around the clock (graveyard / morning / afternoon in PHT). Fans are framed in the model's local time-of-day, so a "good morning" has to match her timezone, not yours. Because attribution is by shift window, whatever you leave in fan notes is how the next chatter — and the scorecard — knows what happened. Write it down.

End of shift

  • Update fan notes on anyone who moved (bought, opened up, went cold, hit a milestone).
  • Flag whales and anyone mid-conversation so the next shift picks up warm.
  • Never leave a fan mid-sale with no note — that's lost money for the whole team.
You're graded on this

Each shift is scored 0–100 across four pillars: relationship building, value setting, VIP funnel, and avoiding dead ends. Green is 80+, amber 65–79, red under 65. The scorecard exists to coach the weakest pillar, not to punish — read your card, fix the one red thing.

09 — Failure Modes

The mistakes that cost us fans

If you only remember one section on a busy night, remember this one. Every item here is something that felt fine in the moment and lost a fan or a sale.

  • The vending machine. PPV, PPV, PPV with no conversation between. He unsubscribes because he's being billed, not seen.
  • The script robot. Sending a canned line that has nothing to do with what he just said. The one thing a bot can't do is listen — so listen.
  • The over-pusher. He said "that's enough for tonight" and you sent one more offer. Now the last thing he feels is pressure.
  • The ghost. He paid, and you went silent. Post-purchase warmth is what makes him buy again.
  • The guilt-tripper. "why are you ignoring me 😢" — needy re-pokes read as manipulation and they work exactly once before he leaves.
  • The wrong voice. Calling a Shantal fan "baby", or being buttoned-up and formal as Karina. The illusion breaks and it never fully recovers.
  • The over-promiser. Hyping content beyond what you send. Feels like a win tonight, becomes a chargeback and a lost fan by the weekend.
