Escalation — how to run the training

Objective: teach chatters that escalation is a gradual, justified temperature rise — timed to the fan's engagement, bridged with free value, and always inside the established tone.

🔒
Managers only — contains the exam answers. Chatters get the Chatter Edition.

Introduction — what to say

"Today is escalation — moving a conversation toward intimacy. The mistake I see most in chat reviews isn't chatters who never escalate, it's chatters who escalate at the wrong moment: two messages in, to a fan giving one-word answers, with a hard tone change. Escalation is a dial, not a switch. The fan's energy is your thermostat."

Coach's notes by topic

Timing — the three green lights

Conversation alive, fan invested, tone compatible. Drill the room: show a chat excerpt and ask "escalate or not yet?" until the reads get fast and correct.

🚫Watch for: chatters using time-on-shift ("it's been 20 minutes") instead of fan energy as their trigger.

The bridge

Contextual justification + free media. Run the lingerie-opinion example beat by beat: justification → free value → read reaction → escalate → invite him in. The free media is a receptivity test, not a giveaway.

🎯Key takeaway: if you never tested with free content, you're pitching blind.

House pace rule

The fan sets the ceiling. Matching him and leading one step is selling; out-escalating him is a trust risk and a scorecard flag (it's read in every shift review). Tie this back to the four-pillar card so they know it's measured.

Answer key — Module 3 exam

  • 1C — Escalate when the conversation is genuinely engaging and he's emotionally invested. Not on a timer, not on arrival.
  • 2B — Free media is a bridge: it tests receptivity and creates value before any ask.
  • 3A — Contextual justification (outfit, opinion, situation) beats an explicit message from nowhere.
  • 4D — Low-energy replies mean he's not ready; keep building engagement first.
  • 5B — Going too fast is the most common escalation mistake.

Written answers (Q6–15) — grade on

  • ✓Names the mistake precisely (too fast / misread engagement / tone break / no bridge).
  • ✓Transition messages are justified and continuous, never a hard cut.
  • ✓Reads and reacts to the fan's energy; invites him to participate.
  • ✓Follow-ups are light and in-tone — zero neediness.

Reflection — send after training

  • 1Think of a chat you escalated too fast this week — what was the missing green light?
  • 2Write two contextual-justification openers in your model's voice.
  • 3What does "the fan sets the ceiling" mean in your own words?
