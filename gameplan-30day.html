The Chatter Playbook · The 30-Day Gameplan

Thirty days to a closing team

Not a generic course. Every drill in this program comes from OUR chats — the threads, the wins, the money left on the table, and the flags that keep repeating in the nightly scorecards. Four weekly themes, one drill per day, measured on the dashboard.

How this program runs

  • 1One theme per week, one drill per day. The Monday routine sends the week's module; the daily drill goes into each chatter's channel with the morning check-in. Fifteen minutes of intention per shift, not homework.
  • 2Everything is measured where it already lives. Exams score the knowing (dashboard), nightly scorecards score the doing (four pillars). A drill "counts" when it shows up in a live thread, not when the exam is passed.
  • 3The real threads are the textbook. Each drill names the actual moment from our chats it exists to fix or repeat. Coaching line: "here is your own thread, here is your own exam answer — make them match."
  • 4Daily non-negotiables, all 30 days: huddle on before the bell · fan notes updated at handover · one seed planted per shift · zero restricted words · holds are a full stop.

The IRAE Method — the spine of everything

Every fan on the page is somewhere on one path: Intrigue → Rapport → Attraction → Enslavement. Every drill in the 30 days serves one of these stages. Know which stage your fan is in, and you always know your next move. Skip a stage, and every later move gets harder.

Stage 1 · INTRIGUE

Make him curious before anything else

The first messages decide whether he invests. Most pages open with "hey how are you" — so the fastest way to stand out is to not sound like a page at all. Intrigue means he closes the app still thinking about her.

Step-by-step procedure:

  • 1Read before you type. Profile, notes, how he found the page. Ten seconds of reading changes the whole opener.
  • 2Open with energy + a sliver of her life. One real detail ("my day was looong") makes her a person instantly.
  • 3Plant a small mystery. "you caught me mid-something 🙈" — an unfinished thought he has to pull on.
  • 4One easy question. His name, or a playful either/or. Never an interrogation.
  • 5Log everything he gives back. The intrigue stage is also the first notes harvest.

Script · the cold open (new sub):

Model · energy + sliver + mystery + easy questionheyyy you have perfect timing, i JUST got back from the most ridiculous shoot of my life 😅 i need to tell someone about it… but first, what's your name?
Fanhaha hi, it's Marcus. what happened at the shoot??
Model · he's pulling the thread — intrigue completeMarcus 😌 okay so imagine me, a very serious concept, and a wind machine that had other plans… i'll tell you the rest but you have to promise not to laugh at me

Why it works: he asked the question. The moment HE pulls the thread, intrigue is done — she never had to chase.

Scenario script · the re-open (fan quiet for days):

Model · mystery re-open, zero needinessokay confession: i almost messaged you yesterday… then i talked myself out of it. now i'm annoyed i did, because the thing i wanted to tell you got even better today 😌
Fanlol okay now you have to tell me

Why it works: "i almost messaged you" flatters without begging; the withheld story forces the reply. Compare with the needy version ("why haven't you messaged me 🥺") — banned.

You've completed Intrigue when: he replies fast, asks a question back, or pulls on the mystery himself. Stage killers: "hey how are you", instant menu-dropping, giving the whole story away with nothing left to chase.

Stage 2 · RAPPORT

Become a person, not a page

Rapport is where the fan decides she's real. Everything sold later stands on this stage — a fan with rapport forgives a miss, waits for payday, and comes back tomorrow. A fan without it is one bad offer from gone.

Step-by-step procedure:

  • 1Bounce off every detail. His job, his team, his weekend — every detail he gives is a door. Walk through it.
  • 2End every message with an open loop. He should never be left holding a message he can't answer.
  • 3Human first on personal shares. Sick dog, hard week, big meeting — the person gets answered before anything else exists.
  • 4Harvest the money rhythm quietly. Payday, spending signals, what he reacts to — into notes, never asked directly.
  • 5Set up tomorrow's callback. "how did the interview go" is next shift's most valuable message. Write it down today.

Script · the detail bounce:

Fanlong week. work's been brutal
Model · human first, then a doorugh i'm sorry 😔 brutal how — busy brutal or people brutal? those are very different problems and i have very different sympathy levels for each
Fanhaha people brutal. my manager mostly
Model · alliance + callback plantedsay no more, we hate him now. officially. okay new rule: this chat is the no-manager zone 😌 what would you actually be doing right now if this week didn't happen?

Why it works: she took his side (alliance), made an insider rule (the no-manager zone becomes a recurring joke), and opened a fantasy door — three rapport tools in two messages. "Manager trouble" goes into notes for tomorrow's callback.

You've completed Rapport when: he shares unprompted, asks about HER day, and returns without being messaged first. Stage killers: "ok cool", interrogation questions, selling into a personal share, forgetting what he told you yesterday.

Stage 3 · ATTRACTION

Raise the temperature until HE asks

Attraction converts the trust from rapport into desire — gradually, justified by the real conversation, and always read against his energy. The goal is never to sell him something; it's to make him ask what it costs.

Step-by-step procedure:

  • 1Check the three green lights. Chat alive, fan invested, tone compatible. No green lights, no escalation — stay in rapport.
  • 2Create contextual justification. An outfit, an opinion, a situation from the actual conversation that naturally leans intimate.
  • 3Bridge with free value. One tease that tests receptivity and gives before it asks.
  • 4Read the reaction, then take ONE step. His response is the thermostat — a dial, never a switch.
  • 5Plant the paid tier without pitching it. "the rest are… a different category 😏" — then let the silence work.

Script · the full attraction arc:

Fan · rapport already builtjust got back from the gym, dead 😅
Model · mirror + shared contextlook at you being disciplined 😌 i just got out of the bath myself, so we're both in recovery mode
Fanhaha yours sounds way more relaxing
Model · justification + mysteryit was… maybe too relaxing. i took some photos before i got in and now i can't decide if they're cute or dangerous 🙈
Fanokay i need to see these
Model · free bridge + tier planted[free tease] just one 😏 the rest are a different category entirely… how's your heart rate, still gym-level?
Fan · HE asks — attraction complete…what's in the other category and what does it cost me

Why it works: every step grew out of the real conversation. He asked the price — which means the sale that follows starts from desire, not from a pitch.

Scenario script · flipping a compliment:

Fanyou're honestly gorgeous
Model · receive, flip, teaseyou're going to make me blush and it's not even noon 😌 careful with that — the last time someone flattered me this well i showed them something i probably shouldn't have…
Fanoh? and what was that 👀

Why it works: the compliment becomes the justification. He's now chasing a story SHE controls.

You've completed Attraction when: he asks to see more, asks the price, or escalates the flirtation himself. Stage killers: escalating on a red-light fan, the hard tone cut, pitching before the bridge, out-escalating where he actually is (the fan sets the ceiling — always).

Stage 4 · ENSLAVEMENT

He can't imagine his day without her

The final stage isn't a trick — it's devotion built deliberately. An enslaved fan opens the app for HER, not for content. He buys without being pitched, defends the relationship ("this is the best part of my day" — a real quote from our threads), and treats spending as part of showing up. This is where whales are made, and it's built from five ingredients:

Step-by-step procedure:

  • 1The ritual. End every good conversation with an implicit appointment ("tell me how the early start goes tomorrow evening — don't make me chase you"). Daily contact becomes a habit HE keeps.
  • 2Memory as intimacy. Open with his details from notes — Max, the meeting, payday. Being remembered is the drug; the notes file is the supply.
  • 3Exclusivity made real. "you're the only one who gets this" — and it must actually be true. First looks, custom words, his name in her world.
  • 4Insiders. Recurring jokes, a nickname earned (not defaulted), references only the two of them share. The relationship gets a private language.
  • 5Attention follows investment — warmly. Her time is valuable and he feels it: she's never desperate, never infinitely available, and the relationship visibly deepens when he shows up for her. Scarcity of HER is what keeps the chase alive inside devotion. Never guilt, never punishment — gravity, not pressure.

Script · the daily ritual (a devoted regular):

Model · memory + ritual, notes: Max, big meetingokay it's been exactly one week and i need my updates: how's Max feeling, and did the big meeting go the way you wanted?? i've been wondering all day 😊
Fanwow you remembered both… Max is back to normal and the meeting went great actually
Model · exclusivity + celebration frameof course i remembered, you're not exactly forgettable 😌 okay this calls for a celebration… and it just so happens i saved something this week that nobody else has seen. tonight, you and me?
Fanwouldn't miss it. 8pm?

Why it works: memory opened the door, exclusivity framed the offer, and HE booked the appointment. That's devotion: he schedules his evening around her.

Scenario script · relationship mode after a big spend:

Model · day after a $200 night — sells NOTHINGno agenda today 😊 i just remembered your presentation was this morning and i've been curious since breakfast… how did it go??
Fanyou have no idea how nice it is that you asked. it went really well

Why it works: the no-sell day after a big spend is the deepest devotion move there is. It proves the relationship is real — and it's exactly why the NEXT premium offer lands bigger. (From our chats: Jaco is what happens without this; the Sam ladder is what happens with it.)

You've reached Enslavement when: he messages first daily, buys without a pitch, keeps the appointments himself, and says things like "best part of my day." Stage killers: becoming the free girlfriend (all warmth, no value frame), 24/7 instant availability (kills the gravity), guilt or punishment plays (kills the fantasy and the account — banned), and letting a handover erase his history (a devoted fan who has to reintroduce himself is halfway out the door).

How IRAE maps onto the 30 days

Week 1 trains Intrigue + Rapport (openers, loops, notes, callbacks). Week 2 trains Attraction (thermostat, bridges, pricing the peak). Weeks 3–4 train Enslavement (rituals, exclusivity, whale protocol, the chase inside devotion). Ask of any thread, any day: what stage is this fan in — and did my last message move him forward or backward?

Foundations — the conversation is the product

Week 1 · Days 1–7 · Modules 1 + 2

Theme: never lose a fan to silence or a closed loop. From our chats: concerns answered with offers instead of answers, flagged VIPs sitting unread, and "ok cool" replies killing warm threads.

Day1

The objection map

Everyone re-reads Module 1. On shift: name every no you receive out loud in notes — price, timing, content, or stalling — before you reply.

DrillLog 3 objections with your diagnosis and the reply you chose. Manager spot-checks the diagnosis, not the outcome.
Day2

Answer the concern first

When a fan raises a doubt or question, the sale waits. Answer like a person, then sell.

DrillOne thread today where you answer a real concern BEFORE any offer — flag it in notes for the scorecard.

From our chats: the Karl thread — his concern got an offer instead of an answer, and the thread died.

Day3

Open loops only

Every message ends with something he can answer. No closed endings, no "thank you 😊" full stops.

DrillRewrite your three most-used replies so each ends in an open loop. Post them in your channel.
Day4

The notes harvest

Discovery is a harvest: identity, life, money rhythm, preferences, pending callbacks.

DrillFive fans get complete notes tonight — including payday if it surfaces. Handover quality is the score.
Day5

The callback

Nothing proves he's talking to a person like being remembered.

DrillOpen two conversations today from NOTES, not from the menu — his dog, his meeting, his week.

From our chats: the JackieD win-back — name used, workout thread ridden, $275 landed because she felt seen, not sold to.

Day6

Revive the one-word fan

"good u" is not a dead fan — it's a fan waiting for something to react to.

DrillHand a story with a hook to three low-effort fans. Track which ones wake up.
Day7

Week 1 review

Objection map + open loops + notes = the foundation. Dashboard check in the handover huddles.

DrillEach chatter brings their best answered-concern thread to the huddle. Two minutes each.

Heat & Money — price the peak

Week 2 · Days 8–14 · Modules 3 + 4

Theme: the hottest minute of the night never passes without a number on it. From our chats: fans begging at the peak and getting hype back, hours of free heat with no ask, and purchases that were never acknowledged.

Day8

Read the thermostat

Green, amber, red — his energy decides the move, not the clock.

DrillMark three threads G/A/R in notes before acting on them. Manager compares your read to the outcome.
Day9

Price the beg

When a fan is asking for it by name, the next unlock is already in his hand — priced.

DrillToday, every "i want it / show me / please" gets a priced reply within one message. No hype without a number behind it.

From our chats: Sam begging at the peak of the teacher scene — the reply was hype only, then two silent hours. Same night he'd already proven a $300 ladder works.

Day10

No free bonfires

Hours of hot chat with zero ask is a bonfire of money.

DrillAny thread hot for 20+ minutes gets one soft priced ask at the peak. One per thread, confidently.

From our chats: Samo — engaged for hours, fully free, not a single PPV, tip ask, or VIP mention.

Day11

The hook, not the file

Captions sell the experience — him, the conversation, the feeling.

DrillCaption surgery on your three most-sent PPVs: cut the specs, add him, add anticipation. Post before/after in your channel.
Day12

Close the loop after a purchase

A fan who just converted is the easiest fan to build on — acknowledge first, always.

DrillEvery unlock today gets a thank-you/after-care line before ANY new topic or offer.

From our chats: Carlos paid $9.99 and came back to a generic good morning — no acknowledgment, no follow-up. That moment went unused.

Day13

The temperature check

Right after an unlock, ask before you drop the next one.

DrillUse one temperature-check line today ("that already sounds like plenty to enjoy tonight… still feeling it?") instead of a back-to-back PPV.

From our chats: Danny — two more PPVs stacked right after his queued unlock, neither opened.

Day14

Week 2 review

Peaks priced, loops closed, bonfires lit with a purpose.

DrillEach chatter brings one priced-peak thread and one after-care thread to the huddle.

Keep & Grow — the VIP machine

Week 3 · Days 15–21 · Module 5 + VIP funnel

Theme: hold value, protect whales, and always offer a middle step. From our chats: shifting VIP prices in the same thread, caution labels read after four sends, big spenders squeezed after they cooled, and warm stalls answered with a pause instead of a step.

Day15

The ladder, in order

Acknowledge and hold → bonus instead of discount → discount only as a last-resort favor.

DrillEvery price pushback today climbs the ladder in order. Log which rung closed it.
Day16

One price, said twice

A shifting number reads as a negotiation you're losing.

DrillPick your VIP ask, phrase it once, and repeat it identically anywhere it comes up all shift.

From our chats: Bohus asked HOW to join VIP and the price flipped between one $100 tip and two $50s — the thread ended on an unanswered nudge.

Day17

The middle step

When a warm VIP says no to the top number, the move is a smaller yes — never a pause.

DrillPrepare your middle step BEFORE every big ask today (preview clip, part one, shorter set with a price).

From our chats: Aussie — six hours warm, opened $189, stalled at $400, and got "wait until you're ready" instead of a $75 preview.

Day18

Labels before sends

Check the caution label the way you check spend history — before the first send, not after the fourth.

DrillLabel-check every thread you open today. If flagged, bond only and loop the manager in.

From our chats: Tim — a NO SALES VIP label, four unlocks in before the mid-shift check caught it. The recovery was right; the order was wrong.

Day19

Land the big spend softly

After a whale spends big and cools, the session ends warm — appreciation, door open, zero further asks.

DrillAny $75+ spend today gets a soft landing line and nothing else that session.

From our chats: Jaco — a beautiful $35→$59→$99 ladder, then squeezed after he cooled, and he never wrote back.

Day20

House rules of the tiers

SVIP is $1k same-day — no installments. VIP bundles CAN be walked through or split, with a small SVIP taste on completion.

DrillSay the tier rules from memory in your channel. Then pitch one mid-spender the correct next tier.

From our chats: Leigh's Sam save — the $100 installment path on a VIP bundle kept an $800 spender in motion. That's the play, on the right tier.

Day21

Week 3 review

Ladders climbed in order, labels read first, whales landed softly.

DrillName your top three spenders and one personal detail each — without opening notes. If you can't, that's the week's lesson.

The Chase — no conversation ends on a no

Week 4 · Days 22–28 · Module 6 live

Theme: the CEO directive becomes muscle memory. From our chats: threads going near-silent after a no, warm spenders flagged three times and never re-contacted, and the exam answers that prove everyone already knows better.

Day22

The loaded pipeline

Never pitch without knowing your next two moves: smaller door, angle change, curiosity plant, takeaway, booking.

DrillWrite your two next-moves in notes BEFORE each big pitch today. If the no surprises you, you weren't ready.
Day23

Energy louder than the no

After a no, the chat gets MORE alive — full sentences, her personality up.

DrillCount your messages after each no today. If the fan out-talks you post-no, you lost the play.
Day24

The takeaway

Raise the bar, never lower the price. He should fight to prove he deserves it.

DrillOne takeaway play today on a hesitating fan, in your model's voice. Screenshot it to your channel.
Day25

The circle-back

A warm spender who went quiet gets one more playful re-contact before shift end. Every time.

DrillEnd-of-shift sweep: every fan who spent today and went quiet gets one circle-back line.

From our chats: Chris — spent ~$50, flagged warm three separate times, and never heard from us again that shift.

Day26

Book the no

A genuine "not tonight" gets a payday, a day, a time — in his thread and in your notes.

DrillEvery real "can't tonight" today becomes a booking. Friday's follow-through is the graded part.
Day27

Holds are a full stop

The second a hold lands, typing stops on that thread — not even a friendly line — until the all-clear.

DrillConfirm the hold protocol in your channel in your own words. One sentence.

From our chats: the Peter thread — offers kept going out after two explicit holds. Never again.

Day28

Week 4 review

Pipelines loaded, energy up, circle-backs swept, holds respected.

DrillBring your best post-no close of the week to the huddle. The team votes the sharpest one.

The point of the 30 days
