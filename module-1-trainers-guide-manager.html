The Chatter Playbook · Module 1 · Trainer's Guide

Mastering the Comeback — how to run the training

Module objective: teach chatters to stop treating objections as the end of a sale. Every objection gives information — their job is to identify the obstacle and guide the fan toward another path to yes.

🔒
Managers only. The chatter-facing version is the Chatter Edition — send them that one.

Introduction — what to say

"Good morning everyone. Today's topic is objections. One thing I notice when reviewing chats is that conversations end too early. A fan says 'It's too expensive', 'Maybe next week', or 'I already have that' — and we immediately stop trying.

Here's the biggest takeaway from today's training: most objections are NOT rejections. They're information. The fan is telling us what's stopping him from buying. If we understand the obstacle, we can choose the right direction instead of ending the conversation."

The four objection types

💰 Price

💡 Coach's note

When a fan says "that's too expensive", don't hear "I don't want it." Hear "I want it, but not at this price." The goal is to remove the price barrier, not defend the price.

💬 Demo in the session

Fanthat's expensive
Badokay babe ❤️
Betteri totally get it ❤️ that's my biggest bundle. i have something smaller i think you'd enjoy just as much
🚫Common mistake: ending the conversation immediately after the objection.
🎯Key takeaway: identify the obstacle before deciding your next reply.

⏰ Timing

💡 Coach's note

"Maybe later" usually means "not yet." The goal is to create a reason to come back. Ask when, remember payday, and leave the conversation warm.

💬 Demo in the session

Fanmaybe next friday
Replyperfect ❤️ i'll save this for you. message me friday and i'll make sure it's waiting
🚫Common mistake: a flat "okay" with no follow-up plan.
🎯Key takeaway: book the return and note payday in the fan notes.

🎥 Content

💡 Coach's note

"I already have that" isn't a rejection of spending — it's a request for something different. Find out what he wants instead of pushing the same content.

💬 Demo in the session

Fani already bought that
Replywhat are you in the mood to see today? i might have something new for you ❤️
🚫Common mistake: arguing "trust me it's different" instead of asking.
🎯Key takeaway: ask before offering another PPV.

💬 Stalling

💡 Coach's note

Some fans keep chatting without moving forward. Build rapport, then confidently ask. If they still don't move, exit gracefully and keep the relationship positive.

💬 Demo in the session

Fanhaha maybe
Replyno pressure ❤️ i just thought you'd enjoy this one. if not today, i'll have something fun for you another time
🚫Common mistake: chatting endlessly with no ask — attention with no direction.
🎯Key takeaway: lead the conversation; one confident ask, then exit warm.

Golden rules to close on

  • ★Don't react to the objection. Understand it first.
  • ★Every objection is information.
  • ★Never assume the conversation is over after the first "no."
  • ★Ask before assuming what the fan wants.
  • ★Always leave another path to yes.
"Fans rarely tell you exactly why they aren't buying. Listen carefully — most of the time they'll tell you the answer. A chatter who understands the reason behind an objection will outperform someone who only memorizes scripts."
