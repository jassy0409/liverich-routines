The Chase — how to run the training

Direct CEO directive: chats are dying at the first no — energy drops, words dry up, buyers walk. Objective: chatters never end a conversation on a no, always pitch with a loaded pipeline, and convert through the chase (confidence, scarcity, takeaway) instead of retreat OR begging.

🔒
Managers only — contains the exam answers. Chatters get the Chatter Edition.

Introduction — what to say

"This one comes straight from the top, and I want everyone to hear it the way it was said: every chat that gets reviewed shows the same pattern — the second a fan says no, we let go. The energy drops, it's barely any words, and a buyer who came here to spend walks away to spend somewhere else. A no could mean many yeses. From tonight, a no is where the real conversation starts. You will always have the next thing ready, and you will make him chase — with confidence and scarcity, never with begging. I need your exam answered today, not tomorrow."

Coach's notes by topic

The silent death

Pull real examples from your chat reviews: show a thread where the words dried up after a no, count the messages before vs after. Make the cost visible. The failure isn't a bad message — it's the missing ones.

🚫Watch for: chatters who think "respecting the no" means abandoning the conversation. Warm persistence and full-energy chat is not disrespect — silence is.

The loaded pipeline

Drill the five next-moves until they're reflexes: smaller door, angle change, curiosity plant, takeaway, booking. Exercise: you say a fan's no, they answer with their next move in under ten seconds, in their model's voice.

🎯Key takeaway: if a no can surprise you, you pitched unprepared.

The chase vs begging — hold this line hard

Pressing means a NEW angle with HIGHER energy. The sad-emoji ladder ("come on 🥺… why are you ignoring me 😢") is begging — it kills the fantasy, burns the fan, and stays a scored dead-end on the shift card. The takeaway play and honest scarcity get everything the begging tries to get, and they actually work.

🎯Key takeaway: never the same door twice. New door, more confidence, every time.

Answer key — Module 6 exam

  • 1B — A no is a direction, not a wall: no to this offer/price/moment, almost never to her. One no can hold many yeses.
  • 2C — The next move is loaded before the pitch: smaller door, angle change, curiosity plant, takeaway, or booking.
  • 3A — Confidence, honest scarcity, and the takeaway make him chase. Begging kills desire.
  • 4D — After a no the energy goes UP. The quiet fade is how buyers walk to another page.
  • 5B — Pressing = new angle, new value, higher energy. Pressure = same ask repeated with guilt.

Written answers (Q6–15) — grade on

  • ✓The reply after a no opens a NEW door (never repeats the declined ask).
  • ✓Energy and word count stay high — personality turned up, in the model's voice.
  • ✓Takeaway replies raise the bar instead of lowering the price.
  • ✓"Can't tonight" gets a full conversation plus a planted seed or a booking — never a flat "no worries" and silence.
  • ✓Zero begging, zero guilt, zero sad-emoji ladders.

Reflection — send after training

  • 1Find the last chat where you went quiet after a no. Write the next-move message you should have sent.
  • 2Which of the five next-moves is missing from your game? Use it tonight.
  • 3Write one takeaway line in your model's voice and keep it ready.
