The Sale

A script is never a copy-paste block — it lives inside emotional continuity. The same pitch can land brilliantly or die completely depending on when and how it arrives.

1 · Discovery2 · Escalation3 · Sale4 · Negotiation5 · Retention

The three-beat sales sequence

  • 1The hook — media + desire. The paid content arrives with text that creates want and emotional urgency. Not a description of the file; an invitation to an experience.
  • 2The reinforcement — ~30 seconds later. One short message that amplifies anticipation ("okay maybe i shouldn't have sent that… 😈"). It plays on the excitement of the moment.
  • 3Light follow-ups — max two. If he goes quiet, one or two playful nudges that show you're waiting for his reaction ("are you still there…? 👀"). In the same tone as the chat. Then stop.
🚫

House line — no guilt ladders

Some agencies teach five sad-emoji follow-ups until the fan feels bad. We don't. Needy guilt-poking ("why are you ignoring me 🥺") is a scored dead-end on your card and burns fans. Two light nudges, then let it breathe — an unanswered PPV is still sitting in his inbox tomorrow.

Sell the feeling, not the file

Same content, two captions

✕ A file

Modelnew video, 5 minutes 🥵 unlock?

✓ An experience

Modeli can't take it anymore, look what you made me do… 🥵 i filmed this thinking about exactly what you said last night. just for you — and i promise you'll be replaying it tomorrow

Why it works — desire, exclusivity ("just for you"), a callback to the actual conversation, and a promise about how it'll make him feel. The content is the vehicle; the emotion is the product.

  • ★Create curiosity. Sell it as something special and private. Curiosity makes him need to see it.
  • ★Personalize. His name, his fantasy, the thing he told you yesterday. Generic captions read as mass messages.
  • ★Tease what's next while sexting. Say things that point toward the next piece before you send it — a teaser beats "hold on a sec."
  • ★Deliver what you promised. Over-hyping content you can't back up feels like a win tonight and becomes a chargeback and a dead fan by the weekend.

Between two sales

The moment a fan buys is exactly when most chatters ruin the next sale.

✕ The vending machine

Fanwow. that was incredible 🔥
Modelglad you liked it! i also have a 10 min version for $40, want it?

✓ The relationship

Fanwow. that was incredible 🔥
Modelmmh i knew you'd love that one 😌 i was actually nervous sending it… now tell me, what was your favourite part? i want details

After-care: exchange a few real messages, maybe a small free bonus, keep the relationship alive. He should feel the connection continues — not that he entered a sales machine. That feeling is what funds the next pitch. (Laddering after a hot "yes" is different — that's one offer at the peak, covered in the manual.)

Caption surgery — file to feeling

Same vault content, three rewrites. The pattern: cut the specs, add him, add anticipation.

✕ "10 min shower video 🥵"

✓ "the shower was cold but i wasn't… i had company in my head, and he looked a lot like you 😏"

✕ "new lingerie set, 12 pics"

✓ "i bought this set thinking 'he's going to lose his mind'… was i right? there's only one way you'll know 🙈"

✕ "sexy video unlock for $25"

✓ "i almost didn't film this one. then i remembered what you told me last night and… well. blame yourself 😈"

✂️

The surgery checklist

Does the caption mention him or your conversation? Does it create a question only unlocking can answer? Does it promise a feeling, not a format? Three yeses — send it.

When the yes is hot — the ladder moment

After-care applies to a satisfied ending. But when his energy is still climbing after a purchase, that's not the end of the sale — it's the middle.

Fan · seconds after unlockingokay that was insane. what else do you have 👀
Model · he asked — ladderoh you're greedy tonight and i love it 😈 there's a part two. longer, and it goes further. since you just treated yourself, $25 instead of $35 if you grab it right now
Fanyes. obviously yes

Read the difference: HE reopened the store. Laddering when he asks is service; laddering when he said "that was amazing, goodnight" is pressure. The signal decides, every time.

Golden rules of the sale

  • ★Hook → reinforce → max two light follow-ups. Then breathe.
  • ★Every caption sells an experience, never a file.
  • ★Personalize with what he actually told you.
  • ★After a purchase: after-care first, next pitch later.
  • ★Never promise more than the content delivers.
