The Chatter Playbook · Module 2 · Trainer's Guide + Answer Key

Discovery — how to run the training

Objective: teach chatters that the first 5–7 messages decide whether a fan invests in the relationship. They must drive the conversation, personalize everything, and delay selling.

🔒
Managers only — contains the exam answers. Chatters get the Chatter Edition.

Introduction — what to say

"Today is about the first messages with a new fan. In 5 to 7 messages he unconsciously decides whether to invest in this relationship. When I review chats, the ones that die fastest all look the same: cold openers, one-word replies, question-question-question like an interview. Today we fix that. The rule: you are the engine. Every message you send either opens a loop or kills the thread."

Coach's notes by topic

The four fatal mistakes

Demo each with a real (anonymized) chat from your own review pile: the flat "u good / wyd" chat, the passive chatter, the generic copy-paste, the interrogation. Ask the room to name what's wrong before you tell them.

🚫Watch for: chatters who think "responding fast" equals "engaging." Speed with dead content is still dead.

Open loops + bouncing

Run the age-question and IT-job examples from the Chatter Edition. Key teaching point: same information collected, completely different emotional result. Every detail the fan gives is a door — walk through it.

🎯Key takeaway: never leave the fan a message that closes the conversation.

No selling yet

Discovery is investment. A horny new sub asking "can I see more?" is still not the moment to dump the menu — we sell on curiosity and mystery. Selling too early reads as a bot and kills the thread.

🎯Key takeaway: connection first; the pitch belongs to Escalation and the Sale.

Answer key — Module 2 exam

  • 1B — By the end of discovery the fan feels listened to, valued, and at ease. That trio is the whole goal.
  • 2C — Stringing closed questions is the interrogation: cold, mechanical, zero connection.
  • 3A — Every message carries an open question or a line that naturally invites a reply.
  • 4D — Bounce off what he said with interest and a playful open question; never "ok cool."
  • 5B — Not yet. Discovery builds the connection the later sale depends on.

Written answers (Q6–15) — grade on

  • ✓Names the actual mistake (flat/passive/generic/interrogation).
  • ✓Reply bounces off what the fan gave and opens a loop.
  • ✓Warm, personalized, energetic — would pass as the model's voice.
  • ✓No pitch, no pressure, no closed endings.

Reflection — send after training

  • 1Which of the four fatal mistakes do you catch yourself making?
  • 2Rewrite your usual opener so it creates energy.
  • 3Find one detail a fan gave you this week that you failed to bounce off.
