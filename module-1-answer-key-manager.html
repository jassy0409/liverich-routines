Part 1 · Multiple choice

The exam auto-scores these five and reports the total to Slack. Use the notes below when reviewing a miss.

Question 1

A fan says, "That's too expensive."

B · He likes the content but not the current price.

Why

When a fan replies instead of ignoring the PPV, there's usually still interest. The objection is about price, not the content. Goal: find another path to yes, not defend the price.

Coach: if they pick A, remind them silence is a stronger rejection than an objection.

Question 2

A fan says, "Maybe next Friday."

C · Book the next opportunity and follow up.

Why

"Maybe next Friday" is a timing objection, not a rejection. A good chatter builds a follow-up plan instead of hoping the fan comes back on his own.

Coach: watch for chatters who just say "okay" and never follow up.

Question 3

"I already bought something like that."

B · Ask what content he'd like to see.

Why

He isn't rejecting spending — he's rejecting that particular content. Asking uncovers what he actually wants and keeps the conversation moving.

Coach: get them to ask before offering another PPV.

Question 4

Which is an example of stalling?

C · "Haha maybe."

Why

Stalling is when the fan keeps chatting without deciding. The chatter should gently guide toward the ask instead of chatting endlessly.

Coach: look for confidence in asking for the sale without being pushy.

Question 5

The biggest mistake after an objection?

C · Ending the conversation immediately.

Why

Assuming the first objection is the end of the sale. Every objection gives information that can be explored.

Coach: objections are opportunities, not stop signs.

Part 2 · Spot the mistake (Q6–9)

Graded by hand. More than one good answer exists. Give full credit if the chatter names the obstacle and explains how they'd keep the conversation moving.

6
Price. Mistake: accepting the "no" and closing with "okay babe." Should acknowledge, then offer a smaller/other option.
7
Timing. Should have booked the return — asked when payday is and offered to save it — instead of ending warm-but-dead.
8
Content. "Trust me it's different" argues. Better: ask what he's in the mood for and find something he doesn't have.
9
Stalling. Chatting 30 min with no ask. Should build rapport then confidently ask; if no movement, exit warm.

Part 3 · Write your reply (Q10–13)

No perfect script. Grade each reply on the five points below.

✓
Acknowledges the fan's concern.
✓
Identifies the real objection (price / timing / content / stalling).
✓
Moves the conversation forward.
✓
Avoids sounding pushy or desperate.
✓
Leaves another path to yes.

Part 4 · Reflection (Q14–15)

Measures understanding, not wording. Look for evidence they grasp that objections are buying opportunities and can say how they'll apply it in real chats.
