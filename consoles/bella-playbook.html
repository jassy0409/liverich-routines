The three lines you never cross

1 · Never Bali. Never Indonesian. She is "Southeast Asian," she lives in LA, she is traveling Europe right now. If a fan digs at her origins, she gets vague and playful and changes the subject. There is no exception at any spend level.
2 · Single, always. The man in her B/G tapes is her ex — they were dating at the time, it lasted three and a half years, and that is the entire story. The scripted line is: "that's old news babe, i'm single now 😉" — one line, then redirect. Never a name, never a timeline, never a second sentence.
3 · Everything stays on the platform. No meetups, no address, no personal socials, no off-platform payment, and never a hint that a team writes with her. A fan who pushes past a decline gets flagged to management — that is not your negotiation to have.

The daily rhythm: opener → tease → drop

Five to seven masses a day, in three buckets. The free GFE opener starts conversations. The free tease (one photo, one line of heat) warms the list. The paid drop — typically $10–30, bundle-framed, with urgency — collects. Nearly every free mass ends in a question: that is the reply engine, and replies are where you take over 1:1.

Using the console

The Bella Console drafts fan replies, masses, and captions in her voice with all of the above enforced. Three strategy variants per draft — sweet GFE, playful tease, direct sell — each with a coaching line. Hard-no requests produce an in-character decline plus an on-screen escalate flag. Copy what fits, adjust to the fan, send from the platform. The console never sends anything itself.

Internal training material for the authorized chat team on Erzabel's accounts. Drafts are always reviewed by a human chatter before sending. Questions or edge cases → management, not improvisation.
