02 — THE METHOD
The IRAE Framework
Every profitable conversation moves through four stages. You don't rush them
and you don't skip them — a fan pushed to stage four while still in stage one just
closes the app. IRAE is the spine of the house style; internalize the order.
I
INTRIGUE
Intrigue — make him curious before anything else
Who is this? New sub or returning whale? What did he say, what's his name,
what's his timezone, what has he bought before? Check fan notes and
history first. The opening line is different for a fresh trial link than for a VIP
who tipped $200 last week.
Goal: know who you're talking to and what he's likely here for before your
first reply.
R
RAPPORT
Rapport — earn the right to sell
Use his name. Ask a real question and react to the real answer. Find one
true thing to connect over — his day, his job, the game he mentioned. This is
the stage most chatters skip, and it's the one that makes every later sale
easy. Two-way conversation, not a monologue with a price tag.
Goal: he feels like he's talking to a person who remembers him.

A
ATTRACTION
Attract — build the tension, don't dump it
Now you raise the temperature: tease, hint, make him curious about what's
behind the lock. Attraction is anticipation. You describe just enough that he
wants more, and you let him ask. A tease that he chased is worth ten unlocks
you shoved at him.
Goal: he is the one leaning in and asking "how much?"
E
ENSLAVEMENT
Enslavement — from buyer to devoted regular
Present the offer with confidence and a clean price. After a yes, ladder up —
a bundle, an add-on, the next tier — while the moment is hot. After a
satisfied done, stop. Escalation includes knowing when the sale is complete
and switching back to warmth so he leaves happy and comes back.
Goal: the sale closes, value is maximized, and he becomes a devoted daily
regular — rituals, memory, exclusivity. The full stage-by-stage IRAE
procedures live in the 30-Day Gameplan.

03 — THE CRAFT
Best chatting technique
The habits that separate a top earner from a warm body with a keyboard. None
of these are tricks — they're just what genuine, attentive attention looks like at
scale.
Open a loop, don't close one
End messages that invite a reply. A question, a tease, a "guess what happened
today" beats a statement he can't respond to. Every closed loop is a dead thread;
every open loop is a fan still typing.
Match his energy, then lead it
If he's shy, be gentle first. If he's blunt and ready, don't waste his time. You mirror
where he is, then you set the direction. Leading from the wrong starting point is
why fans go cold.
Sell the experience, not the file
"A 4-minute video" is a file. "The one you'll be thinking about at your desk
tomorrow" is an experience. Describe how it'll make him feel, never the resolution
or the runtime alone.
Price with confidence, once

07 — COMPLIANCE & SAFETY
The rules that keep the accounts alive
One careless message can get a model banned or a payment reversed. These
aren't suggestions — they protect the creator's income and yours. Read
OnlyFans' own policies (linked in Resources) and treat them as the floor.
Platform hard rules
Never move a fan off-platform. No requests to pay by other apps, meet in
person, or take content elsewhere. It violates the Terms and kills our recourse if
something goes wrong.
Nothing illegal or impersonating. No content involving minors (real or implied
age-play framing), no non-consensual themes, no pretending to be someone
you're not off-platform.

===== PAGE 11 =====
Only sell what exists and is cleared. Don't promise custom content the model
hasn't agreed to. Release forms and consent are tracked for a reason.
Deliver what he paid for. Non-delivery is the fastest route to a chargeback and
a dispute.
Chargeback discipline
A fan who feels tricked disputes the charge — and a high chargeback ratio
threatens the whole account's standing with the platform. Honest teasing sells;
misleading "bait and switch" (promising more than you send) creates disputes.
Protect the ratio by never overselling.
Explicit content: how we handle it internally
Threads get explicit — that's the platform. Two rules govern how we treat that
content in coaching and records:
Never reproduce explicit messages in reports, notes, or cards. Coaching
scores a thread on its structure and outcome — who escalated, did the sale
land, was a signal read — never by quoting the content.
Match, don't over-drive. Let the fan set the pace and meet him there. A chatter
who pushes further than the fan wanted is both a trust risk and a coaching flag.
Reading who's leading whom is a judgment you make every thread.
GOLDEN RULE If you'd be uncomfortable with a manager reading the exact message back to you,
don't send it. Escalate anything genuinely off (a fan mentioning being underage,
threats, self-harm, real-world coercion) to your manager immediately — do not
handle it alone.
08 — ON SHIFT
How a shift actually runs
The operational side of the craft. Consistency across shifts is what makes a fan
feel like he's talking to one continuous person, even though three chatters cover
a day.

===== PAGE 12 =====
Start of shift
Read the handover / recent fan notes so you know where each active thread
was left.
Work the VIP-tagged fans first — they're the priority and they notice speed.
Check what's already been sent from the scheduled queue so you don't repeat
auto-content as if it were live.
Continuity & attribution
Shifts are covered around the clock (graveyard / morning / afternoon in PHT). Fans
are framed in the model's local time-of-day, so a "good morning" has to match her
timezone, not yours. Because attribution is by shift window, whatever you leave in
fan notes is how the next chatter — and the scorecard — knows what happened.
Write it down.
End of shift
Update fan notes on anyone who moved (bought, opened up, went cold, hit a
milestone).
Flag whales and anyone mid-conversation so the next shift picks up warm.
Never leave a fan mid-sale with no note — that's lost money for the whole team.
YOU'RE GRADED ON THIS Each shift is scored 0–100 across four pillars: relationship building,
value setting, VIP funnel, and avoiding dead ends. Green is 80+,
amber 65–79, red under 65. The scorecard exists to coach the
weakest pillar, not to punish — read your card, fix the one red thing.
09 — FAILURE MODES
The mistakes that cost us fans

The vending machine. PPV, PPV, PPV with no conversation between. He
unsubscribes because he's being billed, not seen.
The script robot. Sending a canned line that has nothing to do with what he
just said. The one thing a bot can't do is listen — so listen.
The over-pusher. He said "that's enough for tonight" and you sent one more
offer. Now the last thing he feels is pressure.
The ghost. He paid, and you went silent. Post-purchase warmth is what makes
him buy again.
The guilt-tripper. "why are you ignoring me 😢 " — needy re-pokes read as
manipulation and they work exactly once before he leaves.

formal as Model B. The illusion breaks and it never fully recovers.
The over-promiser. Hyping content beyond what you send. Feels like a win
tonight, becomes a chargeback and a lost fan by the weekend.

The psychology of a "no"
Before the techniques, understand what an objection actually is — because
your emotional read of it decides everything you type next.
1 A reply is interest. A fan who truly doesn't want it ignores you. The moment he types "too
expensive" or "maybe later," he has invested energy in the conversation — silence is the real
rejection, an objection is a door left open.
2 He's telling you the obstacle. Price, timing, content, or momentum. He rarely says it directly,
but it's always in there. Your first job is diagnosis, not persuasion.
3 Don't take it personally. Chatters who feel rejected either go cold ("okay.") or push hard
(needy). Both lose. The objection is about his wallet, his week, or his mood — not about you.
4 Your reply speed matters. An objection answered in seconds feels like a conversation;
answered in twenty minutes it becomes a decision he already finalized alone.
Full worked threads
Short examples teach the line; full threads teach the rhythm. Read how each
one breathes.
